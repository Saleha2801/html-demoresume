Kerala, often referred to as "God's Own Country," is a picturesque state in southwestern India known for its lush green landscapes, tranquil backwaters, serene beaches, and vibrant cultural heritage. From the misty hill stations of Munnar and Wayanad to the scenic backwaters of Alleppey and Kumarakom, Kerala offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and tradition. It is famous for its classical dance forms like Kathakali, rich festivals like Onam, Ayurvedic wellness therapies, and delicious cuisine featuring dishes like appam, puttu, and fish curry. With a harmonious mix of temples, churches, and mosques, Kerala is also known for its spiritual diversity and peaceful living. The state's warm hospitality, wildlife sanctuaries, tea plantations, and unique houseboat experiences make it a must-visit destination in India.
